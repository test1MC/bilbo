Machine Homepage: Screen Overview

[Go Back]

  1. Employee/Machine tabs: Used to switch between the homepages for each Machine at which you are stationed, and to your employee homepage.
  2. Metrics Panel: Displays efficiency and other statistics about this Machine.
  3. Detail Panel: Displays three different information reports about the currently selected step on the Activity List. Choose a tab to switch between reports.
  4. Activity List: A chronological list of the work scheduled at the Machine
  5. Toolbar: Use these buttons to interact with this Machine.

Machine Homepage: Metrics Panel

[Go Back]

Choose the Machine’s tab.

 

The Metrics panel is located on the top-right of the Homepage.

 

As indicated in the panel, these metrics reflect the last 24 hours of activity at this machine.

Description of fields:

OEE

 

The Overall Equipment Efficiency. This is the product of the other three numbers.

Availability

 

The percentage of time over the last 24 hours the machine was actively producing parts.

Productivity

 

How close to finishing jobs at the expected time.

Quality

 

Out of the parts produced, this is the percentage that were good. 100% means that only good parts were produced.

[Go Back]

Description of the reports:

This report describes all the steps involved in the order that this job is part of.

This report lists all the good and scrap parts reported by Operators while working on this job.

Step

 

This report displays special instructions that might have been included with the order.

Start Run

[Go Back]

Indicate the job you’re working on in the Activity List.

 


Choose the START RUN button.

 

If you are already running any jobs, the Console will finish them automatically. You will be prompted to enter the  quantity of parts you produced and to indicate whether or not the job is now complete.

  1. Indicate how many parts were produced.
  2. Indicate whether or not the job is complete.

 

The job’s row in the Activity List will turn green. The START RUN button also becomes the STOP RUN button.

 


Finish Run

[Go Back]

Choose the job you want to finish on the Activity List.

 

 

Choose the FINISH RUN button.

 

 

You will be prompted to enter the  quantity of parts you produced and to indicate whether or not the job is now complete.

  1. Indicate how many parts were produced.
  2. Indicate whether or not the job is complete.

 

 

The job’s row will turn purple to indicate the machine is now idle.

 

 

Report Quantity

[Go Back]

Choose the job from which you want to report parts on the Activity List.

 

Choose the REPORT QUANTITY button.

 

Use the onscreen keypad to enter the quantity of good pieces you’ve produced and choose OK.

 

The job’s QTY (Quantity Produced) and DUE (Quantity due) columns update to reflect the pieces you’ve produced.

 

Pause

[Go Back]

Choose the tab for the Machine that you’ve paused.

 

Choose the reason for the Machine’s pause from the list and choose OK.

 

A new, red-colored row appears on the Activity List indicating the machine’s paused state.

 

Links

[Go Back]

When you choose a job on the Activity List that contains special instructions, the LINKS button will activate.

 

Choose the LINKS button. The Links window appears.

 

Switch between the different items that are attatched to the job by selecting from the list on the left. The information appears on the right.

 

When you’re done, click DONE.

Request Absence

[Go Back]

Choose the REQUEST ABSENCE button. The Request Absence scren appears.

 

Choose the Starting Date drop-down box and indicate the day on which you will be absent or, if you’re taking two or more days off in a row, the first of these days.

 

Indicate the rest of the information about your absence under Amount of absence:

  • If you’re taking more than one day off, chose Absent thru and indicate the last day of the absence in the drop down box.
  • If you’re taking one full day off, choose Just the one day.
  • If you’re only taking part of the day off, chose Only part of the day and indicate how many hours you’ll be absent in the Hours text box.

 

Under Then choose the absence type, indicate the kind of absence time you will use for this absence.

 

Choose OK.

 

Your new planned absense will appear in the ABS (Planned Absences) tab on the Employee Homepage’s Detail Panel. Its status will change to APPROVED or DENIED after your Supervisor reviews it.

 

After your Supervisor has made a decision, you will receive a message the next time you log into the Console. When the messenger icon changes from grey to green, choose it.

 

The message will contain the Supervisor’s decision. Choose DELETE to remove the message, and choose OK to return to the Console.
